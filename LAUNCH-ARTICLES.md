## Beat 1: Materials & Fab (5 articles)

### 1. TSMC's $56 Billion Year: Inside the Largest Semiconductor Capex Budget in History
**Format:** Deep Dive
**Source basis:** TSMC Q4 2025 earnings (revenue $33.7B, +20.5% QoQ), 2026 capex guidance of $52–56B, N2 ramp timeline, CoWoS capacity expansion to 680K wafers in 2025. TSMC commands ~90% of advanced AI chip fabrication.
**Angle:** Break down where the $56B is going — N2 node buildout, advanced packaging expansion (CoWoS-L, InFO), Arizona/Japan/Germany fab investments. What it means for chip pricing and customer allocation.

### 2. TSMC Arizona Turns Profitable: What the First US Advanced Fab Means for Reshoring
**Format:** Deep Dive
**Source basis:** TSMC Arizona subsidiary reported first annual profit. Fab 21 (N4) yields 4% higher than Taiwan fabs (announced Oct 2024). Second fab construction complete, tool install planned for later 2026, HVM in H2 2027. Third fab broke ground April 2025, could enter production as early as 2027 — one year ahead of schedule. Total investment $65B+.
**Angle:** The financial milestone, yield achievement, accelerated timeline for fabs 2 and 3, and what this means for the US semiconductor supply chain.

### 3. ASML's High-NA EUV Rollout: Intel First, Samsung Second, TSMC Waiting
**Format:** Supply Chain Map
**Source basis:** Intel deployed first High-NA system (Twinscan EXE:5200B) in Dec 2025 for 14A process. Samsung received first High-NA scanner late 2025, second in H1 2026, deploying on 2nm lines for Exynos 2600 and Tesla AI chips. SK Hynix installed High-NA at DRAM fab in Sept 2025. TSMC will skip High-NA through A16 (1.6nm) and possibly A14 — their engineering team found alternative approaches. ASML reported €32.7B revenue in 2025 (+16%), record Q4 orders of €13.2B.
**Angle:** Map who has which machines, what they're using them for, and why TSMC's decision to skip High-NA reshapes the competitive landscape.

### 4. ASML Enters Advanced Packaging: The $50 Billion Opportunity Beyond EUV
**Format:** Deep Dive
**Source basis:** ASML shipped its first advanced packaging lithography system (TWINSCAN XT:260, i-line) in late 2025. The advanced packaging market is estimated at $40–50B annually. TSMC holds 50%+ of CoWoS market. Alphabet reportedly reduced 2026 TPU production targets due to limited CoWoS access because NVIDIA secured priority allocations.
**Angle:** Why ASML is expanding beyond EUV, the CoWoS bottleneck that's constraining AI chip supply, and how lithography for packaging creates a new competitive front.

### 5. Japan's Semiconductor Revival: TSMC Expands AI Chip Production in Japan
**Format:** Wire Dispatch
**Source basis:** TSMC announced expansion of AI chip production in Japan (Feb 5, 2026). Japan semiconductor strategy includes Rapidus 2nm partnership with IBM. TSMC Japan fab part of geographic diversification strategy.
**Angle:** Short dispatch on the Japan expansion, context on Japan's broader semiconductor revival ambitions.

## Beat 2: Chips & Architecture (5 articles)

### 6. NVIDIA GTC 2026: Vera Rubin in Production, Feynman on the Horizon
**Format:** Deep Dive
**Source basis:** GTC 2026 scheduled March 16–19 in San Jose. Jensen Huang confirmed at CES 2026 (Jan 5) that Vera Rubin entered full-scale mass production. Rubin features HBM4 memory (288GB), new NVLink interconnect, Transformer Engine, Vera CPU. Feynman architecture expected on TSMC A16 (1.6nm) with silicon photonics — first optical data transmission in a GPU. Jensen teased "never-before-seen" technologies.
**Angle:** Comprehensive preview/analysis of what Rubin delivers, what Feynman signals about the 2028 roadmap, and the supply chain implications of each architecture.

### 7. The HBM4 Race: SK Hynix, Samsung, and Micron Battle for NVIDIA's Memory Orders
**Format:** Deep Dive
**Source basis:** SK Hynix mass-producing HBM4 for Rubin, expected to supply ~2/3 of NVIDIA's total HBM4 demand in 2026. Capacity increase delayed from Q2 to Q3 2026. Samsung officially shipped HBM4 on Feb 12, 2026, mass-producing for Rubin. Micron met NVIDIA specs, delivered final samples, targeting 15K wafers for HBM4 by end of 2026. NVIDIA requested 16-Hi HBM4 devices by Q4 2026. SK Hynix unveiled 16-layer HBM4: 48GB capacity, 2+ TB/s bandwidth.
**Angle:** Three-way race for NVIDIA's wallet. Production timelines, technical specifications, and what 16-Hi HBM4 means for next-gen GPU design.

### 8. Meta's Custom Silicon Roadmap: From Iris to Santa Barbara
**Format:** Deep Dive
**Source basis:** MTIA 2i deployed at scale, reducing TCO by 44% vs GPUs. MTIA v3 "Iris" (designed with Broadcom) in broad deployment as of Feb 2026, custom-tuned for recommendation systems (Reels, Instagram). Inference-only variant "Arke" developed with Marvell. MTIA v4 "Santa Barbara" planned for H2 2026 — first Meta chip with HBM4 and liquid cooling. Target: 35% of inference fleet on MTIA by end of 2026.
**Angle:** Meta's multi-generation strategy to reduce NVIDIA dependency. The economics of custom vs. merchant silicon, and what the Broadcom/Marvell design partnerships signal.

### 9. Alphabet Loses TPU Production Slots as NVIDIA Locks Up CoWoS Capacity
**Format:** Wire Dispatch
**Source basis:** Alphabet reportedly reduced 2026 TPU production targets due to limited access to TSMC CoWoS capacity, because NVIDIA secured priority allocations. TSMC holds 50%+ of high-end CoWoS market.
**Angle:** Short dispatch on the allocation conflict, what it reveals about packaging as the binding constraint in AI chip supply.

### 10. The 16-Hi HBM4 Push: NVIDIA's Demand Is Rewriting Memory Roadmaps
**Format:** Wire Dispatch
**Source basis:** NVIDIA requested all three memory suppliers deliver 16-Hi HBM memory by Q4 2026. SK Hynix, Samsung, and Micron all began full-scale development. SK Hynix 16-layer device: 48GB, 2+ TB/s bandwidth. This represents a significant acceleration of HBM roadmaps.
**Angle:** Dispatch on NVIDIA's demand pull reshaping the memory industry timeline.

### 14. Japan's $26 Billion Data Center Paradox: Hyperscaler Demand Meets Power Constraints

### 15. Microsoft's $80 Billion Problem: When AI Demand Outpaces Infrastructure
